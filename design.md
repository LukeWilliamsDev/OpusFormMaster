# OpusForm — Design Brief

## 1. Product Summary
OpusForm is an operations and compliance portal for a concrete flooring business. It handles crew scheduling, job quoting/invoicing, compliance audit logging, and policy management. This is an internal operations tool, not a consumer-facing product — every screen exists to help a small ops team run field crews and stay compliant.

## 2. Users & Context of Use
- Primary users: internal admins and operations staff.
- Primary device: desktop, used during business hours in an office or job-site trailer setting.
- Usage pattern: data-dense, repeated daily use, not a first-time-visitor experience — optimize for speed and scanability over onboarding delight.
- Compliance-sensitive: audit trails and policy records must feel trustworthy and legible, not decorative.

## 3. Brand Tone
- Professional, industrial-utility, trustworthy, auditable.
- Not playful, not consumer-soft. A clean SaaS feel with a slightly technical/engineering edge.
- Confidence and clarity over personality — the product's job is to be believed and trusted with compliance data.

## 4. Typography
- Single family: **Inter**, weights 400–700.
- Define a type scale: Display, H1, H2, H3, H4, Body, Small, Caption.
- Numerals/tabular data (tables, quotes, invoices) should use tabular figures for alignment.

## 5. Color System
- Leave exact palette open for exploration within the brief above (professional, industrial-utility, trustworthy).
- Required structure:
  - A strong neutral/gray scale (for data-dense tables and backgrounds).
  - One primary accent color.
  - Semantic colors: success, warning, danger, info — used consistently for compliance/audit status states.

## 6. Layout & Page Inventory
Redesign covers the full app:
- Portal shell / navigation (sidebar + layout)
- Dashboard
- Roster view
- Calendar (week view)
- Quote / Invoice Builder
- Audit Log + Audit Diff (before/after comparison) view
- Admin Policies
- Map view (location/job-site embed)

## 7. Core Components to Design
- Nav/sidebar shell
- Data tables (roster, audit log)
- Diff/comparison view (audit changes)
- Calendar week grid
- Quote/invoice builder form
- Map embed
- Status badges/pills (compliance & job states)
- Alerts/notifications

## 8. Content & Density Guidance
Data-dense tables are core to this product. Prioritize scanability, alignment, and information density over whitespace-heavy consumer aesthetics — this is a working tool, not a marketing site.

## 9. Accessibility & Responsiveness
Desktop-first, should degrade gracefully to tablet/laptop widths. Standard WCAG AA contrast bar applies given the compliance context.

## 10. Inspiration References
Open — no specific reference products locked in yet. Direction to explore freely within the brand tone above (professional / industrial-utility / trustworthy SaaS).

## 11. Out of Scope
No implementation detail, no component code, no framework/library specifics — this is a visual and UX brief only, intended as input to a design-generation tool.
